Our Mission: Demonstrate how 2 people can tour Europe for 2 weeks for less than $2,000 during the summer.

  • 14 complete days with tons of sightseeing
  • Stay at some excellent and scenic hotels
  • No middle-of-nowhere creepy motels or cheap dinners

Our secret sauce is using reward points from 4 credit cards to offset a major portion of the travel expense.

Five years ago, I took my first European trip, and it was nothing short of amazing. The awesome food at the Hofbräuhaus in Munch, the canal ride in Amsterdam and seeing Checkpoint Charlie in Berlin are experiences I can't wait to return to. I get daily emails from typical European tour operators, and they seem like a great deal at first. However, I did the math and calculated that when you add all the additional expenses, you're looking at a huge pivot from the advertized teaser price (see typical tour tab).

Alternatively, a fellow travel enthusiast using reward points from credit cards, crunched the numbers and showed me how 2 people can tour Europe for 2 weeks for less than $2,000 during the summer. I have to admit, at first, I was doubtful:

  • The airfare alone is around $1,200 per person
  • I'm not sleeping in a tent or flying in the cargo hold

I looked at his numbers and it does add up. The next few pages will detail an itinerary, introduce the credit cards and show how they profoundly reduce our travel expenses.

Our sample 2-week Itinerary and budget includes these 5 European regions: (see itinerary tab)

  • London (5 nights)
  • Paris, Brussels, or Amsterdam (1 night)
  • Berlin (5 nights)
  • Munich (5 nights)
  • Zurich (1 night)

We hope to show an alternative trip that will include Italy and Switzerland in the future. For now, let’s roll with this itinerary.

We plan it out ourselves; good guidebooks and the internet make it all possible.

Using the rewards from 4 credit cards offers makes this possible. At the time of this writing (8/20/2023), these offers were available. Different bonuses may be currently offered.

  • Chase Sapphire Preferred Card (current offer is for 60k points as of 8/20/2024)
  • Hilton Honors American Express Surpass Card (still offered as of 8/20/2024)
  • Platinum American Express (still offered as of 8/20/2024)
  • Capital One Venture (still offered as of 8/20/2024)

Flight

Let's dive right in and see how we're going to offset the international flight that costs around $1,200 per ticket during summer.

Currently, United Airlines requires 75,000 – 80,000 miles for a round-trip flight to Europe. Flying into London (from Chicago) will cost 35,000 – 40,000 Miles in May/June plus $5.60. Flying back to Chicago from Zurich in May /June will cost 32,000-40,000 miles plus $35 as of 8/20/24. The Sapphire Preferred Credit card is offering 60K bonus points (which can be converted into 60K miles) when you spend $4K in the first three months of getting the card. This card has a bunch of other incentives, but for now let's focus on the points. You get 3 points per dollar when you spend at restaurants and 1 point per dollar elsewhere. Needing around 150,000 miles for 2 people (75K x 2), this credit card gets you part of the way there (64K points, 60,000 bonus + 4,000 for spending). So, what will you do to get the other 100K needed? You have a few options. First, there is a referral bonus with the card, currently (8/20/2024) 10K. Second, you can join Mileage Plus Dining, register your Sapphire credit card and earn miles. Let's see how you can eat your way to Europe.

  • 8 total miles per dollar x $95 per week x 50 weeks (5 miles per dollar as a Dining VIP member plus earn 3 points per dollar for using your Sapphire Card)
    		•    38,000
  • First Dine Bonus
    		•           500

    Third, you can go to Mileage Plus Shopping and earn miles. Let's drill down on that.

  • Approx. 4 miles per dollar x $100 per week x 50 weeks (Earnings vary by merchant, approx. 3 miles per dollar thru Mileage Plus Shopping plus earn 1 point per dollar for using your Sapphire Card)
    		•     20,000
  • Approximate bonus (1,000 miles per month)
    		•      12,000

    Fourth, remember that it is very important that you get your 60,000-point spending bonus. I suggest you use the card for other expenses besides Mileage Plus Dining and Shopping.

  • $1,000 per month for 12 months
    		•   12,000

    Adding up all these points you earn with your new Chase Sapphire Credit Card will get you the miles needed for your 2 award tickets to Europe.

  • Bonus for meeting spending requirement
    		•      60,000
  • Referral Bonus
    		•       10,000
  • Mileage Plus Dining (38,000+500)
    		•       38,500
  • Mileage Plus Shopping (20,000 + 12,000)
    		•       32,000
  • Spending on items (besides dining and shopping)
    		•       12,000
    Total     152,500

    You can also buy those additional needed miles if going to the restaurants and the referral bonus is not how you roll. Keep in mind that you must spend $4,000 in 90 days to get the big bonus, (60,000 points) That wasn't too bad: you now have two international tickets to Europe. I think that justifies paying the $95 credit card annual fee! You just saved about $2,400 on airfare.

    My CPA friend, who helped me crunch these numbers, has flown 9 times to Europe on award tickets and has always found seats available (book early). There are taxes and surcharges that you will have to pay on your award travel. On his last trip, he paid $5.60 for his flight to Europe (London) and $35 for the return (Zurich). Therefore, in my budget I will plan to spend $81.20 for 2 people (total) for taxes in addition to the $95 credit card annual fee. (total airfare expense $81.20 +$95 = $176.20)

    However, we did not factor in our last credit card, the Capital One Venture Card. That card offers 75,000 bonus miles when you spend $4,000 in the first 3 months of getting the card. You also earn 2 miles per dollar charged on the card. These miles work a bit differently than the Chase Sapphire Miles mentioned earlier. With the above-mentioned Chase Sapphire Credit Card, you earn points which you can convert to Mileage Plus miles. When you have 60K Mileage Plus miles you can fly on an award ticket to Europe (roundtrip). The Capital One Venture credit card is used another way. The miles that you earn are used to offset costs for travel-related expenses. You can use them to offset an airline ticket purchased. For example, if your airfare costs $600, you would need 60K Capital One miles (the rate is 1 cent per mile). Because I like to travel in the summer and tickets cost around $1,200 during that time, I would need 120K Capital One miles to offset this expense for each ticket. Therefore, I will use my Chase Sapphire to offset my flight expenses and use my capital one to offset my sightseeing and local travel expenses.

    • One or two new credit inquiries per year should not hurt your credit score. Space your new credit cards out so that you have time to achieve the spend requirement to get your bonus.
    • Some sights are very crowded. Read your guidebooks so you know how to skip the line in many cases by buying your city attraction pass or reserve a time slot if possible.

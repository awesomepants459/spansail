  Typical Tour

Looking at a typical email that I get from a tour operator, a 7-day tour of London and Paris costs $1,499 and includes airfare. That would be great. Where do I click and reserve? Or perhaps I should look into the details.

Looking at the itinerary, I see a lot of free time. For this tour (and typically in many tours) I see 4 totally free days and 3 half days on your own. The whole point of the trip is to see the sites, and I want to do a bit more than just take a morning sightseeing excursion every few days. The tour offers additional excursions costing from $70 for a two-hour jaunt to a whole day outing costing $130. Since this tour has 3 half-days free and 4 full days free, I would easily estimate spending an additional $50 on the half days and $100 on the full days.

Also, when I take a closer look, I need to add $100 to that amount because I would depart from Chicago and not New York.

So now let's see how these additional expenses just increased the cost of our trip.

  • Price of advertised Tour
    		•      $1,499
  • Additional excursions Full days (4 x $75)
    		•      $300
  • Additional excursions half days (3 x $50)
    		•      $150
  • Additional fee (travel from Chicago)
    		•       $100
    Total      $2,049

Now here's the bad news: This price is for travel in February. Unfortunately, I have something that gets in the way of that time frame: School. And even if I had a break from school, I don't think walking around with my parka In Europe would be very enjoyable.

There are tours during summer, but they cost more, and we are already at $2,049 for one person, for one week in February.
